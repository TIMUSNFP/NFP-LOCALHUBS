Understanding the Movement

What Are NFP Circles?

Think of NFP Circles as NetworkFP's regional chapters, where NFP members within a geography come together to grow professionally and personally, by learning together and working towards a common goal of Financial Wellbeing for every Family in India.

01

Learn Together

Access peer-driven learning sessions, case studies, and practitioner knowledge that elevates every member.

02

Build Community

Form genuine connections with fellow Personal Finance Professionals who understand your journey and share your values.

03

Collaborate

Exchange ideas, co-create solutions, and tackle challenges together with trusted peers in your region.

04

Grow Professionally

Sharpen your skills, expand your network, and accelerate your practice with community support behind you.

05

Develop Leaders

Step up, share your expertise, and shape the financial planning profession in your city and beyond.

Circle Membership

What Does It Mean to Be Part of a Circle?

Being part of an NFP Circle is a commitment to yourself, your peers, and the profession.

01

Committed to Learning

Learn continuously with peers who face similar challenges and opportunities. Every session is an investment in your professional future.

02

Quarterly Meetups

Meet at least once every quarter with members in your region. Consistent, structured gatherings that build real relationships over time.

03

Buddy & Mentor Support

Be open to learning, mentoring, and being mentored as part of the community. Give what you have. Receive what you need.

Coverage

Where Can an NFP Circle Be Started?

An NFP Circle can be started in any city, region, or locality that has at least 5 active NetworkFP Members.

The goal is to ensure every financial planner in India, no matter where they are, has access to a supportive local community.

Mumbai Pune Bengaluru Chennai Hyderabad Delhi NCR Ahmedabad Kolkata Jaipur Kochi Chandigarh + Your City

These are examples only. Any city or locality with 5+ active members can start a Circle.
